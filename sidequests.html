STEEL PULSE POSTER

A few months ago I found out that Steel Pulse would be performing in Eindhoven in March of 2025, I’ve been a fan of them for a while and this made me think hm, it would be cool to make a poster for them, just for fun you know. I started collecting images of different posters and of them. I first experimented with cutting each member out of an image and sticking them all together but it didn't really work out. I ended up taking a long break from continuing with this to prioritize more important projects, but after finishing the bob marley website for my passion project I was still really inspired, so i decided to go and finish the poster where I added some elements and used techniques I learned during the designing of the bob marley website. I got feedback from another steel pulse fan on the first iteration of the poster and I made a second iteration of the poster.

REFLECTION

I almost didn't finish this mini project of mine, I thought it would be a fun idea but I do think that it might have been a bit unnecessary, instead of starting on a design like this I could have put the time in my portfolio or the client project (Go-IT), nonetheless im proud of what I made.

CARNIVAL QUEEN PAMPHLET FRONT PAGE

TASK

During the last hectic week of semester 3, my cousin hit me up with a request to design a pamphlet cover for her. She’s in the running for Carnival Queen 2025 in Aruba’s big annual carnival. For context, she’s competing against two other girls in this huge show on January 19th, where they have to win in categories like best dress, best performance, best speech, most popular, and overall winner. She’s been prepping for a year, super busy with all the details, so when she asked, I couldn’t say no. She sent me an example from a Teen Carnival Queen contestant, but since she’s in the main/ older category, her vibe had to be more glamorous and mature. She also gave me a picture of herself to use for the cover.

DESIGNING

I created two iterations of the pamphlet cover: one with different colors and another using her theme colors of blue and green. I added overlays like iridescent effects, sparkles, and light reflections. She preferred the theme colors but didn’t like the playful font I used for her name. She liked the idea of a handwriting script in silver, though I was nervous since I’d never made 3D silver text. She also asked me to tilt her picture slightly. After watching a YouTube tutorial, I used Photoshop to make the changes, adding sparkles and tilting her image. I also helped create a background for the info pages for the judges. Thankfully, I finished everything just before my Canva subscription expired.(that same night i finished)

REFLECTION

Yes, taking this mini project was a risk especially during the final week of the semester, where I should be focusing on other things. I genuinely just wanted to help my cousin out and I am glad I did so, it definitely was a struggle balancing finishing and fixing so many projects at a time. I'm glad how it turned out and I definitely think it looks better than even the inspo I based it on(the girl in the inspo’s elbow is literally missing).
