UX DESIGN - WEB DEVELOPMENT - RESEARCH

My Portfolio - This Website

BRIEFING

Just like every semster we were tasked with making a portfolio website to showcase our work through the semester, what made this time different for a lot of students however is the preperation for the internship. I also took this into account which is why I made my layout more professional and simple compared to ther semesters. I really wanted my work to kind of speak for itself, while of course keeping in mind a clear navigation for teachers during assesments. This website needs to contain or lead its stakeholders (my teachers) to all information they need to assess my progress on the learning outcomes.

ACTIONS

I iterated a lot on landing pages, since I really wanted to convey a specific vibe. I was able to land on a design that I liked but was also professional and clean while being easy to navigate.

After getting feedback from a teacher on the importance of fonts you choose I decided to do a bit of research on the use of fonts, and when and how you use them.

I coded this whole website so I also made sure to add comments to clearly explain how certain codes work and why I wrote them. This website is also set up using a Git repository to neatly keep track of all updates on my code. To give a clear overview on where certain information is in the website or where to find other more specific information I added a README, this gives context and helps with navigation.

Finally, since I wanted to be able to potentially use this website for my internship I didn't want to clutter it with too many details. This is why I made a GitWiki, specifically for the current stakeholders (my teachers) to find more detailed information about my process.

APPROACH

To be able to figure out what direction I should go in for the design I made a moodboard to give myself a clearer view of everything. Then I started making different iterations changing some things based on what I saw on other portfolio websites but also examples given on Canvas of other students portfolioâ€™s. My final iteration was made after getting some feedback from a teacher on the layout.

Coding the website started slow but I definitely started to pick things up as I went on. I had a vision of the moving interactive gradient background, but it seemed complicated so I asked a development teacher for advice on how to tackle it. They recommended using three.js so I had to figure out how to do this using youtube videos. I also made use of AI technology (ChatGPT), when I was stuck with getting things to work, for example I prompted it to help me with the colors, since when I tried doing it the colors would show up muddy. It then helped me figure out that I should use more distinct and brighter colors. I was also having trouble getting the gradient to move more smoothly, since I really wanted it to be almost seamless, so I prompted ChatGPT to help me with this as well. And lastly I did the same for finding out how to resize the screen or rather the gradient on different screen sizes.

REFLECTION

Spending so much time figuring out how to make the gradient work definitely set me back a ton time wise, but I managed and I am quite happy with the result. I would also make a moodboard sooner in the design process as this could have helped me avoid making many versions that I did not like.
